---
id: nasalization
title: Nasalization
summary: A tilde over a vowel usually means some of the air is flowing through the nose during that vowel.
---

Nasal vowels show up in several high-value French-origin words and names. The notation often looks intimidating only because the mark is small.

When you see the tilde, do not overcomplicate it. Read the base vowel first, then add the idea that the vowel is nasalized. In many English borrowings, the native and anglicized versions will coexist, so the mark helps you understand the origin-side pronunciation even if English speakers simplify it.
