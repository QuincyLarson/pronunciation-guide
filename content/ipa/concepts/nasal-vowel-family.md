title: Nasal vowel family
summary: French-style nasal vowels come in a few recurring families, so you do not have to treat every tilde word as a one-off mystery.
---

Once you know the tilde means "nasalized vowel," the next useful step is noticing that the nasal vowels themselves repeat in families.

In practical reading, `ɑ̃`, `ɛ̃`, and `ɔ̃` are often enough to stop a French-style word from collapsing into a blur. You do not need perfect accent imitation first. You need to recognize which nasal family you are looking at and keep moving.
