id: accent-labels
title: Accent labels
summary: Different accent labels do not mean the whole system changes; they usually mark a few recurring symbol mappings.
---

When the site shows **US**, **UK**, or **native/local**, read the label before you panic about the symbols. Most of the notation stays stable, and only a few mappings move around.

Typical examples are British `ɒ` where an American broad transcription may use `ɑ`, or British `ɜː` where an American entry may surface `ɝ` or `ɚ`. The label tells you which house variant you are looking at.
