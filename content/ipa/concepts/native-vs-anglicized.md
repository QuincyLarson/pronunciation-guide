title: Native vs anglicized
summary: Many names and loanwords have both a closer source-language form and a common English-media form.
---

Both forms can be useful. A source-language pronunciation respects the word's origin, while an anglicized pronunciation may be the one listeners expect in English conversation or news coverage.

The site treats this as a choice to understand, not a fight to win. Learn to recognize both when the entry provides them, and pay attention to the context in which each variant is appropriate.
