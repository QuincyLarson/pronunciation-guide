id: tap-vs-trill
title: Tap vs trill
summary: Many languages use an r-like sound that is shorter or more rolled than English r, and the distinction matters in names.
---

English `ɹ` is not the only "r" in the world. Spanish and several other languages use a quick tap or a stronger trill, and many learners benefit from first noticing that these are not the same sound as English r.

For this course, the goal is practical recognition. When you meet `r` in a native or local form, read it as a non-English r family sound and listen to the example rather than forcing an English `ɹ`.
