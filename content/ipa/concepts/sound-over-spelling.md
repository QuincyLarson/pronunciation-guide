---
id: sound-over-spelling
title: Sound over spelling
summary: When the spelling and the IPA disagree, trust the IPA first and use the spelling only as a memory hook.
---

Advanced examples become much less intimidating once you stop trying to force the letters to win.

If the spelling suggests one thing and the IPA shows another, the IPA is the better map of what the page wants you to say. You can still use the spelling to remember the word later, but the decoding move in the moment is: trust the sound symbols.
