---
id: r-colored-vowels
title: R-colored vowels
summary: American-style broad transcriptions often fold the vowel and the following r into a single symbol.
---

When you see **ɚ** or **ɝ**, read them as an **r-colored vowel**, not as a mystery extra consonant.

- **ɚ** is the unstressed version, common at the end of words like *father*, *water*, or *computer*.
- **ɝ** is the stressed version, common in words like *bird*, *learn*, or *word*.

This is one of the main notation bridges between British and American dictionary transcriptions. A British entry may show **ɜː**, while an American one may show **ɝ** or **ɚ** in the corresponding spot.
