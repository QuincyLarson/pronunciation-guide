title: Stress and syllables
summary: Stress marks tell you where the word leans, which often matters more than tiny vowel perfection.
---

Longer words become much easier once you stop reading IPA as a flat string. Stress marks show which syllable carries the main beat and which one carries a weaker secondary beat.

If you catch the stress pattern first, the rest of the word becomes easier to decode. A good rhythm with slightly imperfect segment detail usually sounds better than perfect segments with the wrong stress.
