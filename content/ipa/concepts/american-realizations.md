title: American realizations
summary: Some American English pronunciations add taps or glottal stops, but those are realizations, not a separate alphabet.
---

In many American accents, **t** can sound like a tap in words such as *water* or *city*, and some syllables may use a glottal stop in words such as *button*. The site surfaces those patterns because they are common and useful.

The important idea is not "memorize a second spelling system." It is "recognize a common pronunciation shortcut when you see it." Keep the underlying word and stress pattern in view.
