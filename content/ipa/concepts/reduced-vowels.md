id: reduced-vowels
title: Reduced vowels
summary: English often weakens unstressed vowels, and schwa is the symbol you will see most often when that happens.
---

English spelling makes vowels look stable. Spoken English does not. In unstressed syllables, a lot of vowels weaken toward a smaller, less distinct sound.

That is why words like *about*, *sofa*, and *taken* often contain **schwa** instead of a strong full vowel. Reading this well matters more than memorizing terminology. When you see `ə`, think "the vowel relaxed because the syllable is not carrying the weight."
