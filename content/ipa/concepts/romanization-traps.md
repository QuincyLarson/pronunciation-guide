id: romanization-traps
title: Romanization traps
summary: Borrowed names and loanwords often use familiar Latin letters, but those letters may belong to another spelling system, not to English sound values.
---

This is one of the biggest advanced-course traps: you see ordinary letters and your brain tries to read them with English habits.

In Pinyin, Spanish, Japanese romanization, and other systems, the letters may look familiar while standing for very different sounds. The fix is simple: treat the spelling as a clue to origin, then let the IPA tell you the sound.
